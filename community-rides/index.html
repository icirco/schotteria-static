Community-Owned Rides: Guidelines

Gravel. Grit. Community.

The Concept Sometimes there is no official Schotteria Ride planned. Or you just want to spin up a ride with friends. We love the initiative: reach out, plan a route, and get out there.

Route & Planning

  • Use this channel only for community ride planning and communication.
  • Share your route in advance (GPX / Komoot / Strava).
  • Include the basics: when, where to meet, coffee before/during/after, etc.
  • Ensure all roads/paths are legal for cycling.
  • Give a heads-up on distance, elevation, and expected pace.
  • State clearly if it's a drop or no-drop ride‚Äîthere must never be dropping on a no-drop ride.

Ride Format

  • Every ride needs at least one designated captain (you, or someone you nominate).
  • Captain(s) handle navigation and keep things running smoothly.
  • Captain(s) can assign helpers: sweep, mark turns, signal hazards.
  • Do a pre-ride briefing: introduce captain(s), route, signals, and do a headcount.

The Schotteria Spirit

  • These rides represent our community ‚Äî ride respectfully and follow traffic rules.
  • If someone has a mechanical or needs to turn back, make sure they're sorted.
  • If you organize the ride, you're the host ‚Äî be welcoming and social.
